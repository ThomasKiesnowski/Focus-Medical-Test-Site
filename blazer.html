Key Differentiator

"Predictable Cement Delivery —
Even in Sclerotic Bone."

Standard vertebral augmentation systems rely on hydraulic cement pressure to penetrate bone — a technique that fails in hard, sclerotic lesions and chronic fractures. The Blazer-C system takes a fundamentally different approach: a Nitinol wire mechanically creates channels through the vertebral body first, enabling controlled, predictable cement delivery regardless of bone density.

A single unilateral 10G (3mm) entry point achieves complete vertebral body coverage — reducing patient exposure and procedure complexity without compromising fill.

Nitinol Wire Channel Creation
Mechanically creates bone pathways before cement delivery — eliminating the guesswork of hydraulic pressure in dense or irregular bone morphology.

Engineered specifically for blastic lesions, chronic fractures, and sclerotic bone — the cases that standard augmentation cannot reliably treat.

Product Features

Engineered for the Hard Cases

Nitinol Wire Channel Creation

Mechanically creates pathways through bone for predictable cement flow — even in hard, sclerotic lesions where hydraulic augmentation systems fail to achieve adequate fill.

Unilateral Approach

Single-entry 10G (3mm) access achieves complete vertebral body coverage without bilateral access. Fewer punctures, less patient exposure, faster recovery.

Directional Cement Control

Cannulated wire design delivers cement with precise directional guidance through established channels — reducing extravasation risk and improving fill distribution.

Works in Sclerotic Bone

Engineered for blastic lesions and chronic fractures where other augmentation systems struggle. The Nitinol wire overcomes bone density limitations mechanically.

Office or Hospital

Clinical Applications

Indicated Fracture Types

Osteoporotic Vertebral Compression Fractures — Primary indication for vertebroplasty pain relief and stabilization
Malignant Vertebral Fractures — Pathological VCFs from primary bone tumors or metastatic disease
Benign Lesions (Hemangioma) — Symptomatic vertebral hemangiomas requiring augmentation and stabilization
Sclerotic Lesions — The Blazer-C advantage: Nitinol wire penetrates dense, hardened bone where hydraulic systems cannot
Blastic Metastatic Disease — Dense bone-forming metastases that resist conventional vertebroplasty fill techniques
Chronic Fractures — Long-standing VCFs with remodeled, hardened architecture that reject standard cement delivery
