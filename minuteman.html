The Minuteman Advantage

Lateral Fusion Without the Open Approach.

Traditional lateral interbody fusion requires large incisions, psoas retraction, and overnight stays. The Minuteman® G3 achieves fusion through a tissue-sparing corridor with a small tubular retractor — enabling same-day discharge and faster patient recovery.

Product Features

Designed for Surgeon & Patient Outcomes

The Minuteman® G3 combines a minimally invasive lateral approach with interbody fusion — reducing surgical trauma while delivering structural stability.

Minimally Invasive Lateral Access

Access the lumbar disc space through a small lateral incision using a tubular retractor. Avoids the extensive muscle dissection required by traditional open approaches.

Tissue-Sparing Approach

The small working corridor preserves surrounding musculature and soft tissues, resulting in significantly reduced blood loss and less postoperative pain for patients.

Same-Day Discharge

Patients can go home the same day as their procedure. The reduced surgical trauma enables faster mobilization and shorter hospital stays compared to traditional lateral fusion.

Reduced Blood Loss

The tissue-sparing technique through a small tubular retractor dramatically reduces intraoperative blood loss — improving patient outcomes and lowering complication risk.

Small Tubular Retractor

Purpose-designed small-diameter retractor provides adequate visualization and working space while minimizing the surgical footprint and tissue disruption.

Faster Patient Recovery

Minimally invasive access means less muscle damage, reduced narcotic requirements, and earlier return to daily activities — improving the overall patient experience.

Clinical Applications

Lumbar Interbody Fusion via Lateral Access

The Minuteman® G3 is designed for lateral access lumbar interbody fusion in patients who require stabilization and fusion of the lumbar spine.

Degenerative Disc Disease

Patients with symptomatic lumbar degenerative disc disease who have failed conservative treatment and require interbody fusion for structural support.

Spondylolisthesis

Low-grade lumbar spondylolisthesis requiring stabilization. The lateral approach provides indirect decompression via disc height restoration.

Spinal Stenosis

Lateral interbody fusion restores disc height and foraminal dimensions, providing indirect decompression for patients with lumbar spinal stenosis.

Revision & Adjacent Segment Disease

The lateral corridor provides a virgin surgical field for revision cases or adjacent segment pathology, avoiding scarred posterior anatomy.
